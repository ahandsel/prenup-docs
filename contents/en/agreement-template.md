# {{ $frontmatter.title }}

The two individuals entering into this marriage (one hereinafter referred to as "Party A" and the other as "Party B") agree as follows and conclude this agreement.


## Article 1 (Mutual respect)

Party A and Party B shall respect each other's differences in personality, the living environment in which each was raised, culture, values, tastes, and preferences. They shall recognize that Party A and Party B are equals in their married life, and shall love and support each other as lifelong partners.


## Article 2 (The conduct of the married couple)

Party A and Party B vow to each other that, in health and in sickness, in joy and in sorrow, in wealth and in poverty, they shall love, honor, comfort, and support each other; that for as long as they live, they shall love and cherish each other and act with all their heart; and that they shall cooperate to further enrich and develop the lives each has built so far.


## Article 3 (Marriage registration)

Party A and Party B shall each sign and affix their seal to the marriage registration form, and shall file it together no later than [Month] [Day], 20XX.


## Article 4 (Marital property and the sharing of living expenses)

Property that Party A and Party B each owned before the marriage, as well as property acquired after the marriage, shall be the separate property of the respective owner.

1. The living expenses required for the life of the family unit (meaning the couple and their children; the same applies hereinafter) shall be shared fairly between Party A and Party B, taking into account each person's income and their respective shares of the household chores.
2. Any debt that Party A or Party B incurs for the family unit with the consent of the other party, and any debt arising from day-to-day household matters, shall be the joint and several obligation of both parties unless otherwise specifically agreed. Any debt incurred after the marriage without the other party's consent shall be the obligation of the party in whose name it was incurred.


## Article 5 (Division of household chores)

Household chores shall be divided so as to maximize the well-being of the family unit, and each person shall perform the household chores associated with their own basic daily life. However, where both parties agree, or where other circumstances make it necessary for both parties, they shall endeavor to decide the matter through discussion.


## Article 6 (Custody and upbringing of children)

Party A and Party B shall cooperate in the custody and upbringing of their children. Living expenses, education expenses, recreation expenses, and other costs required to raise the children shall be shared fairly between Party A and Party B and carried out, with the utmost respect for the children's welfare and after thorough discussion between Party A and Party B.


## Article 7 (Relationships with relatives)

So long as it causes no harm to the family unit or its members, Party A and Party B shall each cherish both their own parents and the other party's parents, and shall endeavor to maintain good relationships with each other's relatives.

1. As a general rule, Party A and Party B shall not live together with each other's relatives. However, if the need to live together arises due to nursing care or similar circumstances, Party A and Party B shall decide the matter by respecting each other's opinions and discussing it thoroughly.
2. As a general rule, Party A and Party B shall each support their own relatives at their own responsibility and financial expense, and shall share their intention to do so in advance before providing such support.


## Article 8 (Pledges)

Party A and Party B mutually pledge to each other as follows:

1. To disclose their income and expenditures upon the request of the other party.
2. To apply themselves diligently to work and household chores, except in special circumstances such as illness.
3. To always hold respect and affection for the other party, and not to speak ill of the other party in front of their children or relatives.
4. Not to have sexual relations with any third party, and not to engage in infidelity or in conduct that could be mistaken for infidelity.
5. Under no circumstances, regardless of the situation, to commit violence or verbal abuse.
6. To take responsibility for the custody and education of the children.
7. To actively cooperate with each other in household chores such as cooking, laundry, and cleaning.
8. To strive to remain healthy in both mind and body.
9. To engage in gambling, such as pachinko and horse racing, only to an extent that causes no harm to the family unit.
10. Not to stay out overnight without notifying the other party.
11. Not to open or view the other party's letters, email, mobile phone, or the like without permission.
12. Never to borrow money from consumer finance companies or other moneylenders without the other party's consent.
13. Not to engage in criminal acts or in words or conduct that significantly damage the other party's trust.


## Article 9 (Living apart)

If living apart becomes necessary for reasons such as work or medical recovery, the parties shall discuss the matter in advance and obtain the other party's consent. However, this shall not apply where there are justifiable grounds, such as the need to seek urgent refuge.

1. During the period of living apart, Party A and Party B shall each conduct their own life at their own responsibility and expense. The sharing of living expenses during the period of living apart shall be determined through discussion, according to each party's income and other circumstances.


## Article 10 (Divorce)

If either party proposes a divorce, Party A and Party B shall discuss the matter in good faith.


## Article 11 (Provisions in the event of divorce)

* (1) In the event of divorce, Party A and Party B agree as follows:
  * (1-1) As a general rule, each party's personal belongings shall remain the property of that party, and jointly owned property shall be divided so that each party receives one-half, regardless of in whose name it is held.
  * (1-2) If either party caused the grounds for the divorce, or if one party bears greater responsibility than the other, the party who caused the grounds (or who bears the greater responsibility) shall pay consolation money to the other party.
* (2) If there is a child between Party A and Party B, the parties agree as follows:
  * (2-1) If the child is three years of age or older, the parties shall respect the child's wishes in deciding who holds parental authority, and that person shall have custody of and raise the child.
  * (2-2) If the child is from zero to under three years of age, the parties shall decide who holds parental authority through discussion, and that person shall have custody of and raise the child.
  * (2-3) If either party is pregnant, that party shall hold parental authority over the child and have custody of and raise the child.
* (3) If there is a child between Party A and Party B, the parties agree as follows:
  * (3-1) The parent who does not hold parental authority (the non-custodial parent) shall pay child support in accordance with the court's child support calculation standards, from the end of the month containing the later of the date the divorce is finalized or the date of birth, until the month in which the child graduates from a university or similar institution (or, if the child does not go on to a university or similar institution, the month in which the child reaches 18 years of age). The parent who holds parental authority shall give the other party the opportunity to have visitation with the child approximately once per month.
  * (3-2) If there is no biological parent-child relationship between a party and the child, that party shall be exempt from paying child support even if that party becomes the non-custodial parent.
  * (3-3) If either Party A or Party B has committed abusive acts, such as child abuse or domestic violence, the opportunity for visitation shall, as a general rule, not be granted.
* (4) Until the obligations under the preceding items have been fully discharged, if any change occurs, such as a change of job, a change of residence, or a change of contact telephone number, the party shall promptly notify the other party of the change, either before or after it occurs.
* (5) When filing the notification of divorce, Party A and Party B shall, in advance, prepare a notarized deed of divorce settlement reflecting the intent and content of paragraphs (1) through (4) of this Article.


## Article 12 (Exclusion from application)

Party A and Party B mutually confirm and agree that, because this agreement was concluded before the marriage, the "right to rescind contracts between husband and wife" provided for in Article 754 of the Civil Code shall not apply.


## Article 13 (Good faith)

For any matter not provided for in this agreement, where doubt arises as to its interpretation, or where a change in circumstances or conditions makes a review necessary, the parties shall discuss the matter in good faith and endeavor to reach a resolution.


## Article 14 (Amendment of the agreement)

The provisions of this agreement may be amended only by the written agreement of all parties.
